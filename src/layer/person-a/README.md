# Person A (Personality-Centered Campaigns)

**Personality drives relevance** - At the end of the day, what determines whether software is good is how suitable it was to augment or otherwise improve real world behavior. Therefore, the science of how marketable software is must first be a study of how the target market thinks and behaves before a study of the work they do.

- Campaign starting point: Marketing campaigns usually start by asking who this is for and often center on disciplines or job types.
- Personality as the anchor: Personality heavily informs what kinds of work people are suited for, what behaviors they can endure, and their strengths and weaknesses.
- Agentic experiences: For the experiences being built with agentics, personality may be more important than domain or job category.
- Campaign targeting: Campaigns should be centered on personality first, not discipline.
- Exception: Domain targeting only makes sense if the motivations in that domain attract a certain personality type and there is a heavy distribution of that personality in those roles.
- Practical advantage: Personality is a better starting point than persona research because personas are a subset of personality and are usually harder and more expensive to derive.
